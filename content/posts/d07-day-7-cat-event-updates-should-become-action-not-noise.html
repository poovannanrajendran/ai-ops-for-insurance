Day 7: Cat event updates should become action, not noise

When an event breaks, teams need a briefing that says what changed and what to do.

Day 7 turns catastrophe bulletins into impact summaries and actions.

Problem: Dense bulletins slow understanding of impacted classes and likely loss bands. MVP achieved: Tool classifies event, estimates severity, identifies impacted classes, outputs concise actions. Scale path: Multi-feed ingestion, timeline tracking, exposure overlays, escalation workflows.
Should cat briefing optimise for speed, depth, or strict one-page operations?
