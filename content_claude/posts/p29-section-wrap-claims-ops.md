# P29 — Section Wrap: Claims Ops — Four Tools Across the Claims Lifecycle

Endorsement diff. Referral scoring. FNOL triage. Reserve reasoning.

Four tools targeting four different moments in the claims lifecycle. But they share a design principle: enhance the handler's judgment, do not replace it.

**What I learned:**

Claims is where insurance gets personal. A triage decision is not just a routing label — it determines how quickly someone's loss gets attention. A reserve is not just a number — it shapes financial reporting and resource allocation. The stakes of getting these wrong are higher than in most underwriting workflows.

That is why every claims tool returns reasoning, not just results. "Fast-track because: value under threshold, coverage clear, no complexity flags" is a defensible decision. "Fast-track" alone is not.

**The dual-mode pattern worked.**
Day 10's FNOL triage with both email parsing and guided chat intake was more work to build, but it addressed the real-world reality: sometimes you have a broker email, sometimes you have a phone call. The triage engine should handle both.

**Reserve documentation is underserved.**
Day 11's reserve advisor addresses a gap I have seen repeatedly: the initial reserve is set, but the reasoning is not captured in a queryable format. When the reserve is reviewed three months later, nobody remembers why it was set at that level. Documented reasoning changes the quality of the review conversation.

The claims section demonstrated something important: AI tools in claims need to be more careful, more documented, and more transparent than in any other insurance domain. The regulatory and customer-impact sensitivity is highest here.
