# P23 — Section Start: Broking and Placement — The Right Risk Still Needs the Right Market Path

A broker once told me the hardest part of placement is not finding a market. It is telling the right story to the right market at the right time.

The submission quality, the wording precision, the market selection, the timing of approach — these are the craft of broking. And most of them still run on experience, relationships, and manual document preparation.

In Week 3 of my challenge, I will build tools that support broking and placement workflows:

- **Broker Submission Builder** — Guided wizard to structure broker submissions with all Lloyd's market fields
- **Placement Status Tracker** — Kanban board for placement progress with syndicate statuses and AI follow-up suggestions
- **Market Reform Contract Checker** — Paste contract text, flag non-compliance with MRC standards
- **Wordings Diff Tool** — Compare two wording versions, highlight material differences with practical impact assessment

These tools do not replace broker judgment. They reduce the manual work around it — so the judgment can happen faster and with better-prepared materials.

The London Market reform agenda is pushing towards structured data, electronic placement, and standardised wordings. The brokers who adapt fastest will be the ones who can produce clean, compliant, well-structured submissions while maintaining the relationship quality that wins markets.
