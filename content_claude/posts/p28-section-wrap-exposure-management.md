# P28 — Section Wrap: Exposure Management — From Spreadsheets to Spatial Intelligence

Two tools. Two perspectives on the same problem.

The heatmap (Day 6) answers: "Where is our exposure concentrated right now?"
The cat briefing (Day 7) answers: "What does this event mean for our book right now?"

One is proactive surveillance. The other is reactive response. Both require the same foundation: location-aware, value-aggregated, threshold-driven exposure data.

**What I learned building these:**

Maps beat tables for exposure communication. A portfolio report showing "$45M TIV in Florida" communicates differently than a glowing heatmap showing three postcodes on the Gulf Coast carrying 60% of that value. The visual changes the conversation from "we are within tolerance" to "we need to check those three locations."

Cat briefings need to be opinionated. A neutral summary of an event is a news article. A useful briefing says "this affects your marine cargo book in Southeast Asia — here are three actions to take today." The tool needs to take a position, not just present facts.

**The connection to underwriting:**
These exposure tools close the loop on the underwriting section. Day 1's triage copilot decides whether to write a risk. Day 6's heatmap shows whether writing that risk creates a concentration problem. Day 7's briefing shows what happens when the concentration is tested by a real event.

That is the portfolio management cycle: select, monitor, respond. Three tools covering the full loop.
