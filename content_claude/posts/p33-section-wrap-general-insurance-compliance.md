# P33 — Section Wrap: Compliance — Built In, Not Bolted On

Three tools. Three compliance workflows. One design principle: the control should be invisible to the user.

Data quality validation catches problems before submission. Sanctions screening runs at the point of data entry. Regulatory digests surface what changed and whether it affects your book.

None of these require the user to "do compliance." They just do their work, and the compliance happens.

**What I learned:** the resistance to compliance tooling is almost never about the compliance itself. It is about the friction it introduces. A sanctions check that takes 30 seconds and runs in the background gets adopted. A sanctions check that requires opening a separate portal, entering data manually, and waiting for a batch result gets worked around.

The design challenge is not "how do we check sanctions." It is "how do we check sanctions without the user noticing an extra step."

Same applies to data quality. The best validators do not produce a report that someone files. They produce inline corrections that the user accepts or rejects in the flow of their existing work.

The compliance tools in this challenge are deliberately minimal in scope but maximal in integration. They are designed to sit inside existing workflows, not beside them.
