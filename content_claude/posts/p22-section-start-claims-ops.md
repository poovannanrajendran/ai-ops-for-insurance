# P22 — Section Start: Claims Ops — Where Process Meets Promise

Here is a truth that rarely makes it into strategy decks:

The moment a policyholder files a claim is the moment your product is actually tested.

Everything before that — the slip, the placement, the premium — is a promise. Claims is where the promise gets kept or broken. And the experience of that moment shapes retention, reputation, and regulatory standing more than any sales pitch.

Yet claims operations remain one of the most process-heavy, least digitised parts of most insurance organisations. FNOL intake is manual. Triage is inconsistent. Reserve-setting reasoning is undocumented. Handoffs between handlers, adjusters, and panel firms create information gaps that slow resolution and increase leakage.

The irony: claims teams sit on mountains of structured and unstructured data. Every notification, every assessment, every correspondence — it is all recorded. But it is recorded for compliance, not for operational intelligence.

AI can change that equation. Not by replacing claims handlers — the judgment calls in claims are too consequential and too context-dependent for full automation — but by making the operational backbone faster and more consistent.

In this section of my challenge, I built three tools for claims workflows:

1. **Policy Endorsement Diff Checker** (Day 8) — Compare expiring and renewal wordings to catch material changes before they become coverage disputes.

2. **Referral Priority Queue Scorer** (Day 9) — Replace FIFO queue order with weighted priority scoring so reviewers work highest-risk items first.

3. **Claims FNOL Triage Assistant** (Day 10) — Dual-mode intake (email parse or guided chat) with deterministic triage classification and factor-level reasoning.

4. **Claims Reserve Advisor** (Day 11) — Initial reserve suggestions with documented reasoning and synthetic precedents.

Each tool focuses on a specific handoff where speed and consistency matter most.
