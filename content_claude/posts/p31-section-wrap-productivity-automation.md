# P31 — Section Wrap: Productivity — The Gains Come from Removing Friction

The biggest productivity improvements I have seen in insurance did not come from new software.

They came from removing one unnecessary handoff. One redundant data entry step. One report that nobody read but everyone spent four hours preparing.

The tools in this section follow that principle: find the friction point, remove it, and give the time back to the professional who needs it for judgment work.

Renewal strategy memos that used to take half a day to draft. QBR narratives that required collecting data from five sources. Meeting briefings assembled from memory and last week's emails.

None of these tasks require deep expertise. They require data assembly and formatting — exactly the work that AI handles well and humans find draining.

**The lesson:** productivity tools succeed when they disappear. The user should not think "I am using an AI tool." They should think "that was fast."
