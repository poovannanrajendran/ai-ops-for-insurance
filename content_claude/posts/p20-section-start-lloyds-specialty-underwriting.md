# P20 — Section Start: Lloyd's and Specialty Underwriting

The best underwriting teams I have worked with share one trait.

They are not the ones with the most data. They are the ones who reach the right decision fastest — and can explain why.

In the London Market, the underwriting workflow has not fundamentally changed in decades. Broker submits risk. Underwriter reviews slip. Appetite is checked against experience and memory. Terms are negotiated. Stamp goes on.

The steps are sound. The friction is in the gaps between them.

A submission sits in an inbox for three days because nobody triaged it. An appetite check happens from memory because the statement is a PDF nobody can search. A slip review takes 45 minutes because every clause needs manual reading. A referral sits in a queue because there is no urgency signal beyond gut feel.

AI does not replace any of these decisions. But it can compress the time between "received" and "first informed opinion" from days to minutes.

Over the next posts, I will share five tools I built for underwriting workflows in the first week of my 30-day challenge:

1. **Submission Triage Copilot** — broker submission to accept/refer/decline in minutes
2. **Portfolio Mix Dashboard** — concentration and accumulation visible before the next bind
3. **Risk Appetite Parser** — machine-readable appetite from prose documents
4. **Slip Reviewer** — key terms and flagged clauses from MRC slips
5. **Class of Business Classifier** — free-text risk descriptions to structured class codes

Each one targets a specific bottleneck. Each one is deployed and demoable. None of them requires replacing existing systems.

The question for any underwriting team considering AI is not "should we?" — it is "where do we start?"

I think the answer is wherever the gap between receiving information and acting on it is longest.
