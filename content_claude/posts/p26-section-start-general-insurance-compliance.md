# P26 — Section Start: Compliance — The Best Controls Do Not Slow the Business

Compliance gets a bad reputation in insurance.

It is seen as the thing that adds friction, delays decisions, and generates paperwork. And honestly? In many organisations, it does. Because the controls were bolted on after the workflow was designed, not built into it.

The best compliance implementations I have seen — and I have seen many across Guidewire platforms, Lloyd's reporting, and regulatory change programmes — share one trait: the control is invisible to the user. It happens in the flow of work, not as an interruption to it.

A sanctions check that runs automatically when an insured name is entered. A data quality validation that flags anomalies before the file is submitted. A regulatory update digest that tells you what changed and whether it affects your book.

These are not compliance burdens. They are workflow features.

In this section:

- **Data Quality Validator** — Upload any insurance data file. Get schema validation, convention checks, anomaly flagging, and suggested fixes.
- **Sanctions Screening Aid** — Enter insured or entity name. Fuzzy-match against public sanctions lists (OFAC, EU, HMT). Risk flag with confidence and next steps.
- **Regulatory Update Digest** — Lloyd's/PRA/FCA feeds summarised with relevance flagging by class.

The goal: make compliance the path of least resistance, not the obstacle course.
