# P21 — Section Start: Exposure Management — If You Cannot See It, You Cannot Control It

I once sat in a board meeting where someone asked: "What is our total insured value within 50 miles of the Florida coastline?"

The room went quiet. Not because nobody knew the answer — but because getting the answer required pulling data from three systems, reconciling location formats, and building a manual overlay. The "quick check" took two days.

Exposure management is the control tower of any insurance operation. It is where accumulation becomes visible, concentration becomes measurable, and steering decisions become defensible.

But most exposure management in the London Market still relies on periodic snapshots, spreadsheet aggregations, and expert judgment about "how much is too much in one area."

That works until it does not. And when it fails, it fails at the worst possible moment — after a cat event, when the board needs answers that should have been available before the event.

In this section of my 30-day challenge, I built two tools that address the visibility gap:

1. **Exposure Accumulation Heatmap** (Day 6) — CSV-driven location mapping with TIV aggregation, threshold warnings, and AI-generated commentary. See where value is clustering before you cross tolerance lines.

2. **Cat Event Briefing** (Day 7) — Structured event classification with impacted classes, territory mapping, loss estimates, and action items. A first-response briefing in minutes instead of hours.

Both tools share a principle: exposure decisions should be driven by what you can see, not what you remember.

The market reform agenda — Blueprint Two, CDR modernisation — is pushing towards more structured, more frequent, more granular exposure data. The tools that will matter are the ones that make that data actionable, not just available.
